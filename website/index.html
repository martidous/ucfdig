The Journey

Where I Started

My career began in the world of structure and systems—product management, consulting, entrepreneurship, and software development. I built applications, designed strategies, and solved complex business problems. Everything had a framework, a process, a clear objective.

I was good at it. But something was missing.

The Questions

The questions started small: What if technology could tell stories? What if code could be expressive, not just functional? What if systems could surprise you?

I found myself drawn to the edges—where logic met creativity, where data became visual, where interaction became experience. The clean lines of my career path started to blur.

The Transformation

I dove into digital media and creative coding. p5.js became my canvas. WebXR opened new dimensions. Interactive storytelling merged everything I'd learned about structure with everything I was discovering about expression.

The shift wasn't abandoning my technical foundation—it was building on top of it. Every business insight, every system I'd designed, every problem I'd solved became a tool for creating experiences that mattered.

Where I Am Now

Today, I work at the intersection of technology and creativity. I speak both languages fluently: the language of product development and the language of artistic expression. I design with both logic and imagination.

I'm building interactive experiences, teaching artists to code, and exploring the boundaries of what's possible when you refuse to choose between structure and play.
